Printathon

Fall 2015

Date | Brandeis University

On the weekend of March 7th, Deis3D will hold the first Printathon, a 24-hour event in which teams from educational institutions gather and compete to produce a 3D-printed creation attending to criteria divulged at the beginning of the competition by a panel of judges including student leaders, technology specialists and educators.

Some Frequently Asked Questions

How does it work?

You'll work in teams of 3-6 makers to design and 3D print your creation. Only fused filament fabrication (FFF) printers are allowed. Teams will be provided with a theme/challenge during the beginning of the printathon, which although not mandatory, will influence the judgement of your creation. Printing starts on Saturday morning and goes on for 24 hours straight.

What should I bring?

You'll need your student ID to check-in, and we recommend bringing a laptop for slicing and CADing. Teams should also bring their own printers, filament, and printer tools.

How much does it cost?

The event is completely free! We'll provide food and drinks, as well as shared printers to be used throughout the event.

Who can attend?

All students from any college/university or high school are welcome.

What are the judging criteria?

Creations will receive different grades on theme adherence, creativity, complexity, functionality, 3D printed components and presentation.
